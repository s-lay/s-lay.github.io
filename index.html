About Me

My ancestor immigrated from China to Cambodia. As for my generation, I consider myself to be more cambodian than chinese. I was born and raised in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. At the age of nine years old, I moved to Lowell, Massachusetts. As a first generation student, I wasn't properly educated by my family. I learned and figured out things along the way. I wasn't an outstanding student when I was young. I learned the value of education late in middle school. Thereafter, I treated school seriously. In high school, I challenged myself with AP and Dual Enrollment courses. In senior year, I won the Paul Tsonagas Scholarship, a merit full scholarship to Umass Lowell.

Currently, I am a senior Computer Science student at Umass Lowell. Aigoo, it seems like it was recently I entered college. I took my first programming course in junior year in high school. My first programming language was in Visal Basic. I ended up choosing Computer Science as a major because I find it interesting and at the time, I didn't know what I wanted to do. I not only like coding, but also the math and puzzle portion that it consists of. I love the feeling I receive when my code works. It's like a blank wall results in bunch of doors and finally I open enough doors that the light comes in. I have to say this path isn't easy. I experienced frustation, irritation, stress, and the list goes on. There was time I doubted myself whether I fitted into this major or whether I'm smart enough. I learned nothing is impossible, only possible with retrieving determination. Here I am, continue to move forward without knowing my abilities!
